Peptide-MHC binding prediction is solved (NetMHCPan). But the next question — does a specific T-cell receptor actually recognize a given peptide-MHC complex? — is what the field calls "the holy grail of systems immunology," and it's still broken.
Unsolved questions:

Generalization to unseen peptides. ML methods demonstrate high accuracy predicting TCR specificity for known peptides but fail to generalize to novel peptides. bioRxiv Models hit ~0.89 AUC on seen epitopes and crater on unseen ones. The predictive power of all approaches is low on the most challenging zero-shot task, and all TCRcube variants perform very poorly on cancer neoantigen prediction specifically. bioRxiv
Physics vs. sequence representations. Physics-based methods using classical energy functions outperform ML methods when predicting TCR binding to novel peptides, but underperform them on known peptides. bioRxiv This suggests current sequence-based models are memorizing training distributions rather than learning generalizable binding logic. Surface-level biophysical features (electrostatics, shape complementarity) may carry more transferable signal than sequence embeddings.
Structural modeling quality bottleneck. Few TCR-pMHC complexes were modeled with sufficiently high quality to allow for reliable binding assessment, suggesting the structure pipeline was unable to produce models with sufficiently accurately docked TCRs. Frontiers AlphaFold3 helps but doesn't solve it — CDR3 loop conformations remain unreliable.
The negative data problem. The source of negative TCRs substantially impacts model accuracy, with external negatives potentially introducing uncontrolled confounders. Nature The standard practice of generating negatives by randomly swapping TCR-pMHC pairs may be leaking distributional shortcuts into benchmarks.

Entry points: The IMMREP_2022 benchmark dataset is public. A systematic leave-one-peptide-out evaluation comparing surface/physics-based features against sequence embeddings on AF3-predicted structures would directly address the generalization gap. The field is also wide open for someone to properly characterize how negative sampling strategy inflates reported AUCs.
